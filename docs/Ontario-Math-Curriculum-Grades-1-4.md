# Ontario math — grades 1–4: skill content (2020 curriculum)

Working checklist for *Practice Math*. This lists **substantive math content** (operations, ranges, representations, facts)—not SEL, processes, or pedagogy.

**Source of truth:** [Ontario curriculum catalogue — elementary mathematics](https://www.dcp.edu.gov.on.ca/en/curriculum/elementary-mathematics) → download the latest **Grades 1–8 Mathematics** PDF. Use it to verify wording and codes (B1, B2, C1, …).

---

## Grade 1

### Whole numbers

- Read, represent, compose, decompose, compare, and order whole numbers **up to and including 50**.
- Estimate quantities in collections **up to 50** and check by counting.
- Count forward by **1s, 2s, 5s, and 10s** (to 50).

### Addition & subtraction

- **Recall** addition facts with sums **up to 10** and **related subtraction facts**.
- Use mental math strategies (including estimation) to add and subtract whole numbers whose **total or difference stays within 20** (i.e. work within sums/differences **≤ 20** for the combined calculation as specified in the curriculum).

### Fractions

- In **fair-sharing** contexts: recognize **one-half** and **two-fourths** of the same whole as equal.
- Use drawings to **compare and order unit fractions** from fair shares with **up to 10 sharers**.

### Algebra — patterns & equations

- Work with **repeating patterns** and simple **equalities** (structure grows in later grades).

### Algebra — coding

- Write and run code with **sequential** steps; read/change simple code and describe how changes affect the outcome.

### Data

- Sort, collect, display, and read data with **concrete graphs**, **pictographs**, and **tally charts** (and related representations named in the PDF).

### Spatial sense (geometry & measurement)

- Describe and compare **2D shapes** and **3D objects** (attributes, location, movement as in the PDF).
- **Measure length** with non-standard and standard units; work with **time** (e.g. reading time on analog clocks) as specified.

### Financial literacy

- Identify **Canadian coins to 50¢** and **coins and bills to $50**; **compare values** (which is worth more, etc.).

## Grade 2

### Whole numbers

- Read, represent, compose, decompose, compare, and order whole numbers **up to and including 200**.

### Addition & subtraction

- **Recall** addition facts for totals **up to 20** and **related subtraction facts**.
- Use mental math strategies (including estimation) for addition and subtraction problems in the **ranges given in B2** (see PDF).

### Multiplication & division

- Relate **multiplication** to equal groups / **arrays** and **repeated addition**; relate **division** to fair sharing and groups (introductory expectations—exact fact scope is in **B2**).

### Fractions

- Fair-share problems: **up to 10 items** shared among **2, 3, 4, or 6** sharers (results may be whole numbers, mixed amounts, or fractional—see PDF).
- Recognize **one-third** and **two-sixths** of the same whole as **equal** in fair-sharing contexts.
- Compare fractional amounts using drawings; **standard fraction notation** is **not** a grade-2 requirement (words and models first—confirm in PDF).

### Algebra — patterns & coding

- **Repeating and growing patterns**; translate and extend patterns (representations named in C1).
- Coding with **sequential** events; read/modify code as in C3.

### Data

- Sort, display (e.g. **bar graphs**, **pictographs**), and interpret **one-variable** data (exact expectations in D1).

### Spatial sense

- **2D / 3D** shapes and relationships; **length** (metric); introduction to **area** concepts as specified; **time** and other measurement expectations in E1.

### Financial literacy

- **Money amounts** in cents and dollars in simple contexts (e.g. counting coins, simple transactions—see F1).

## Grade 3

### Whole numbers

- Read, represent, compose, decompose, compare, and order whole numbers **up to and including 1 000**.
- **Skip-count** forward and backward by **2s, 5s, 10s, 25s, 100s** in various contexts (confirm lists in B1).

### Addition & subtraction

- Use mental math strategies (including estimation) to add and subtract whole numbers such that the **situations stay within 1 000** (see **B2.3** wording in the PDF).

### Multiplication & division

- **Recall** multiplication facts for **×2, ×5, and ×10** and **related division facts**.
- Represent and solve problems involving multiplication **up to 10 × 10** using models, strategies, and drawings (full **recall of all facts 1–10** is **grade 4**, not grade 3).

### Fractions

- Represent and compare **fractions** using drawings, tools, and standard notation where introduced in B1 (denominators and contexts are listed in the PDF).

### Decimals

- Usually **not** a focus in grade 3 in the same way as grade 4—confirm B1 if your board uses a condensed timeline.

### Algebra — patterns, equations, coding

- **Growing patterns**, tables of values, and pattern rules (C1).
- Coding: control structures beyond pure sequencing as specified in **C3** (e.g. loops/events—verify in PDF).

### Data

- **Bar charts**, **pictographs**, **vertical and horizontal bar graphs**; **likelihood** language (probability) as in D2.

### Spatial sense

- **Perimeter** of polygons; concepts of **area**; **mass** and **capacity**; metric units; **time** and elapsed time as in E1.

### Financial literacy

- **Add/subtract money** amounts in **dollars and cents** in problem contexts (F1).

## Grade 4

### Whole numbers

- Read, represent, compose, decompose, compare, and order whole numbers **up to and including 10 000**.
- **Round** whole numbers to the **nearest ten, hundred, or thousand**.

### Addition & subtraction

- Multi-digit **addition and subtraction** within the ranges and strategies specified in **B2** (mental math, algorithms, estimation—see PDF).

### Multiplication & division

- **Recall** multiplication facts from **1 × 1 through 10 × 10** and **related division facts**.
- Use **standard algorithms** and other strategies for larger computations as specified in B2 (including division with remainders in contexts defined in the curriculum).

### Fractions

- Represent, compare, and order **fractions** from **halves to tenths** using models and **standard notation**; explain **numerator** and **denominator**.
- Fair-share and set models with denominators such as **2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10** (exact list in B1).

### Decimals

- Read, represent, **compare**, and order **decimal tenths**.
- **Round** decimals to the **nearest whole number**.
- Relate **fractions** and **decimal tenths** (equivalence in the sense required by B1).

### Algebra — patterns, equations, coding

- **Repeating and growing patterns**; tables and graphs; rules and predictions (C1).
- Use **symbols as variables** in expressions and equations (C2).
- **Solve equations** with whole numbers **up to 50**; **solve inequalities** involving addition/subtraction with whole numbers **up to 20** and represent solutions (C2).
- Coding expectations in **C3** (more complex control flow than early primary—see PDF).

### Data

- **Many-to-one** pictographs and **bar graphs**; **median** and data spread ideas as introduced in D1; probability language in D2.

### Spatial sense

- Classify **angles** (acute, right, obtuse, straight); **area** of rectangles; relationships between **area and perimeter**; more on **2D/3D** relationships (E1).

### Financial literacy

- Estimate and calculate **costs**, **change**, and **unit pricing** in money contexts (F1).

## How to use this in the app

- Treat each **bullet** as a possible **question type** or **difficulty band** (e.g. grade 1: facts to 10; grade 2: facts to 20; grade 4: all facts to 10×10).
- When you add **fractions, decimals, money, measurement, or data**, map items to the **grade** where that skill first appears or is fully expected.
- Re-check the **PDF** whenever you encode **exact** bounds (e.g. “up to 20” vs “within 20”) so your generator matches the official wording.
